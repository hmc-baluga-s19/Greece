Tourist Destinations

There are some spots that you have to visit while you're in Greece. Below you will find descriptions and images of some of the most popular locations in the country.

The Acropolis : Athens, Greece

Parthenon temple
Erechtheion temple
The Odeon of Herodes Atticus
Acropolis

If you are able to travel to Athens then you must visit the Acropolis. The Acropolis is home to the famous Parthenon, a temple dedicated to the goddess Athena Parthenos. The Acropolis also contains the Erechtheion, a temple famous for a porch sustained by 6 caryatids. Another top attraction at the Acropolis is the Odeon of Herodes Atticus which is an ancient stone theater built by Herodes Atticus in memory of his wife. These and many other beautiful, ancient sites are housed in the Acropolis and are available to visitors for only 20 euros, 30 euros for a more extensive list of sites!



Olympia, Greece

Panathenaic Stadium
Temple of Zeus
Temple of Hera
Ancient Olympia

Olympia is home to the some of the most important archeological sites in Greece. It was the location of the very first Olympic Games which were held every 4 years in honor of the Greek God Zeus. Visitors can go to the western Peloponnese to learn all about the first Olympic Games through exploring the sites. Popular stops iclude the ancient stadium, the gymnasium and the temples of Zeus and Hera. There is also an Archaeological Museum in Olympia where many impresesive artefacts are displayed.



Delphi, Greece

Delphi

The sanctuary of Delphi, a famous ancient oracle, is located at the foot of Mount Parnussus and was a major cultural and religious center during its time. According to mythology, it was the point where two eagles, one from the east and one from the west, thatr were sent by Zeus met. Delphi was also home to the Pythian games which took place every 4 years in honor of Apollo. Delphi is among one of the most beautiful and interesting archaelogical sites in Greece.




Meteora, Greece

Inside the Monasteries

Meteora (which means suspended in the air) is home to 24 14th-century Byzantine monasteries built on top of rock pillars where monks could seek refuge from the Ottoman empire. The area was already a shelter for hermits and ascetics in the 10th century who were looking to live away from the world and closer to God. Temple of Poseidon, Cape Sounio

Temple of Poseidon
Cape Sounio
Cape Sounio
Cape Sounio

At the southernmost tip of the Attica peninsula, 70 kilometers away from Athens, the Cape Sounio is known to be the place where Athenian king Aegeus jumped into the sea to his death when he thought his son Theseus was killed by the Minotaur, giving his name to the sea. But Cape Sounio is more than just a place of legend. It is home to the Temple of Poseidon. While the first version was destoyed by the Persians in 480 BC, the second version, of which a few columns still stand today, was built in 440 BC.



Santorini, Greece

Santorini Volcano

A top destination in Greece, Santorini ranks high among the most romantic destinations in the world. This volcanic island in the Cyclades experienced a severe volcanic eruption, which gave the island its current aspect. Blessed with unique beaches with red or black sand, the island is home to picture-perfect villages on top of the caldera, luxurious beach resorts and a dynamic wine production scene.
